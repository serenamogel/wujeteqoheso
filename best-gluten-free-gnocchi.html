    What’s the best gluten free gnocchi on the market? We ate a dump truck’s worth of dumplings to find the best GF gnocchi worthy of your special jar of marinara—the one you only open for guests. Read on for our ranking of the best gluten free gnocchi brands you can buy. 

    If you’re trying to avoid gluten, pasta can be one of the most frustrating foods to buy, as wheat-based brands greatly outnumber your gluten free alternatives. But gnocchi, those wonderful little Italian dumplings, tend to be more gluten-friendly than their skinny pasta brethren, using alternative ingredients like potato, chickpea, or cauliflower, and a range of different packaging from frozen, to refrigerated to shelf-stable boxes. 

    But with more options comes more confusion, so we took it on ourselves to sample a range of gluten free gnocchi brands, ranking them on taste, texture, and (perhaps most importantly) convenience. So, here are our picks for the best gluten free gnocchi you’ll find in your local stores.

    Best Gnocchi with Sauce

    Caulipower Cauliflower Gnocchi with Savory PestoWalmart

    If you’re feeling lazy, this frozen gnocchi is probably the closest you can get to “instant.” You just throw everything from the bag into a skillet with three teaspoons of water, simmer it all for six to seven minutes, and you’ve got a tasty, saucy gnocchi, ready to plate. The pesto sauce is one of the best parts of this meal, with a strong, savory flavor mixed with frozen tomato pieces that give it a rich texture that you don’t get from a jar. And the cauliflower gnocchi holds up well for being frozen, giving you plenty of bite and not devolving into mush like some other frozen pastas we could (but won’t) mention.

    Credit: Liv Averett / Walmart

    Rating:

    7/10

    Best Boxed Gnocchi

    Delallo Chickpea GnocchiDelallo

    If you need a gnocchi you can keep on a shelf and forget about for a while, Delallo’s offers this shelf-stable gluten free gnocchi that lets you just boil them for a few minutes and toss them on the plate. And unlike some chickpea pastas, it doesn’t go limp after boiling, delivering a solid bite and only a subtle chickpea flavor that betrays its bean-based origins. So, if you need something quick and tasty that will leave space in your fridge, this is the best gluten free gnocchi for you.

    Credit: Liv Averett / Delallo

    Rating:

    7/10

    Sporks

    Most Filling Gnocchi

    DeCecco Gluten Free Fresh Potato GnocchiDeCecco

    This refrigerated gluten free potato gnocchi might not last as long as the frozen or shelf-stable kind, but it definitely delivers a hearty potato taste, possibly because it’s 75 percent potato purée. (But I’m no scientist.) I can say that all that potato gives it a great homemade taste, a light, fluffy bite and a filling sensation as those carbs fill up every inch of your stomach.

    Credit: Liv Averett / DeCecco

    Rating:

    8/10

    Sporks

    Best Refrigerated Gnocchi

    Nuova Pasta Gnocchi Gluten Free Classic PotatoNuova

    Of all the gluten free gnocchi I tried for this list, this refrigerated gluten free potato gnocchi had the shortest ingredients list with just “rehydrated potatoes, canola oil and salt.” But those three ingredients create a simple, tasty GF gnocchi with a great, doughy feel and a rich, homemade taste. (That’s assuming “homemade” doesn’t mean made in “my home,” where the kitchen is just a pile of Del Taco bags and empty sriracha bottles.)

    Credit: Liv Averett / Nuova

    Rating:

    9/10

    Sporks

    Best of the Best

    Cappello’s Restaurant Quality PastaCapello’s

    When they say “restaurant quality, ” they’re not kidding. This was my favorite gluten free gnocchi of all the dumplings I sampled. Now, part of that might come from the cooking instructions, which ask you to pan sear the gnocchi after boiling it. But either way, it’s hard to beat this frozen potato-based gnocchi, which even boasts a slightly nutty flavor, presumably from the almond flour that makes it gluten free.

    Credit: Liv Averett / Capello’s

    Rating:

    9/10

    Sporks
