What is the difference between low e2 and low e3?

18 Feb 2024, 00:00

Answer Generally speaking, the greater the number of layers of low-E coating put to a window, the less natural light is allowed to enter the residence in general. As reported by Milgard Manufacturing Inc., a conventional low-E2 window provides 70 percent visible light (VT), whereas low-E3 windows provide 66 percent visible light (VT). In the same vein, what is a low e2 value? Light and heat are managed through and reflected away from windows by low E coatings, which are microscopically thin and virtually invisible on the glass surface.
