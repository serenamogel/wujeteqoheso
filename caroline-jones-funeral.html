    The funeral arrangements for ABC journalist, Caroline Jones, has not been stated.

    The Jones family are still planning her funeral and will update the public with relevant information in due time.

    Jones, best known as the one who spare headed the show Four Corners, as far back as 1972.

    Jones won a top Australian award for investigating the inner-city slum landlords at the time, and it was just her first year as a journalist.

    In 2013 she was awarded for her outstanding contribution to Journalism, and seven years down the line, Jones was inducted into the Australian Media Hall of Fame.

    Jones was in the media sector for over 5 decades and was a pioneer member of the Australian Council for the Arts,

    Jones announced her stepped down from her role as Australian Story presenter in 2016.

    Jones was married twice during her lifetime but chose not to have children of her own.

    Jones died after a fall in her home. She was 84.
