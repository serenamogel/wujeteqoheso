    Curtis Jones parents-English professional footballer, Curtis Julian Jones was born on January 30th, 2001 in Liverpool, England.

    Jones was born to Sandra Jones and his little-known father. He happens to be the youngest among four children.

    We have no details about his siblings at the moment. He holds British nationality and is considered to be of mixed ethnicity.

    From a young age, Jones became quite passionate about football. The outstanding player he is today is the result of his mother’s encouragement.

    Sandra Jones went with him to the practice field and offered early support for his goals. Jones attended the official school of Liverpool FC, Rainhill High School, in Rainhill, Merseyside, England.

    Curtis Jones career

    When Jones was nine years old, he joined Liverpool. Jones earned his first professional deal on February 1, 2018, following his U23s debut in January 2018.

    He was selected on the substitute bench for Liverpool’s Premier League match against Everton on April 7 but did not take the field.

    Jones had a significant role for Liverpool in the preseason prior to the 2018–19 season. His dribbling abilities and mobility were commended by manager Jürgen Klopp.

    On January 7, 2019, Jones faced Wolverhampton Wanderers in the third round of the 2018–19 FA Cup. This was his first game with the first team.

    On September 25, 2019, in a 2019–20 EFL Cup encounter against Milton Keynes Dons, Jones made his season debut and participated in his second competitive game for the team. He was chosen to be the match’s man.

    Then, in Liverpool’s triumph over Arsenal in the EFL Cup penalty shootout, he converted the game-winning penalty. On December 7 against AFC Bournemouth, he made his Premier League debut as a substitute.

    Jones played for a Liverpool team that defeated rival Everton 1-0 at home in the 2019–20 FA Cup on January 5, 2020. The team was primarily made up of teenagers and reserve players.

    Jones’ 20-yard curling shot was the game-winning goal and his first for the team. Jones scored the first goal in a Merseyside derby since Robbie Fowler scored for Liverpool in 1994 at the age of 18 years and 340 days.

    Jones re-signed a long-term contract with the team on July 4, 2020. The next day, in a 2-0 victory over Aston Villa, he scored his first league goal for the team by latching onto Mohamed Salah’s header and beating former Liverpool goalkeeper Pepe Reina.

    Jones qualified for a Premier League winner’s medal on July 22 when he made his fifth top-flight appearance with Liverpool against Chelsea, coming off the bench.

    Jones was selected man of the match by Sky Sports on September 24, 2020, when he scored two goals in four minutes against Lincoln City on the road in the 2020–21 EFL Cup.

    On December 1st, he scored his first UEFA Champions League goal in a 1-0 victory over Ajax, ensuring Liverpool’s advancement into the competition’s final rounds.

    Jones got his first league goal of the 2020–21 campaign on February 28, 2021, in a 2-0 away victory over Sheffield United.

    On September 25, 2021, Jones netted his first goal of the 2021–22 campaign in a 3–3 away tie with Brentford. He contributed two assists and a crucial pass on September 28 in a 5-1 away victory over Porto in the 2021–22 UEFA Champions League.

    Jones extended his long-term deal with Liverpool through June 2027 on November 17, 2022. Jones scored his first goal of the 2022–2023 campaign on April 30, 2023, in a 4–3 victory over Tottenham Hotspur.

    On May 15, 2023, he maintained his strong play by scoring a quick-fire brace against relegation-threatened Leicester in a man-of-the-match performance to lead Liverpool to a 3-0 away victory and maintain their slim chances of finishing in the top four of the Premier League.
